## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: WearAura does not look AI-generated at the macro level. The dark tricolore background, cream-only accent discipline, and typographic bipolarity (Cormorant/Montserrat) are coherent and deliberate. The feed slide uses `object-fit: contain` on Ink Navy — this refuses the Instagram crop, shows complete outfits, and lets flat-lay photography breathe. That is a design decision, not a default. The nav pill is the app signature component and is well-executed.

At the micro level, the camera step UI echoes Snapchat and Instagram's well-worn layout (gallery thumbnail / capture ring / flip button). No visual decision signals uniqueness there. The settings screen is a standard iOS-style list. Neither is a failure, but they are where "AI-made" could be whispered.

One authentic slip: "WEARAURA" branding in the camera viewfinder uses `letter-spacing: 4px` in Cormorant Garamond at 16px. At that size it reads as a watermark, not a brand moment.

## Overall Impression

The feed is where WearAura earns its concept: atmospheric, editorial, purposefully slow. The path to getting content into that feed — the three-step create flow — is where the experience collapses into a generic form grind. Step 2 asks users to classify type, material, color, and size all at once across a wall of chips. That is the biggest single opportunity: compress creation friction without sacrificing the data model.

The second structural issue is the nav copy-paste problem. Navigation is duplicated six times in HTML, and four copies have zero accessibility attributes. This is an invisible problem today and an expensive maintenance debt tomorrow.

## What's Working

**1. The feed slide composition.** Full-screen `object-fit: contain` on Ink Navy is the right call for fashion photography. The gradient overlay (`rgba(0,0,0,0.25) transparent rgba(0,0,0,0.65)`) keeps text readable without masking the image. This is confident design.

**2. The hotspot mechanic.** Tappable product annotations directly on the photo is a genuinely differentiated feature. The GPU-composited `::after` ripple animation (after Emil fixes) signals interactivity elegantly. This is the core product differentiator.

**3. The settings FAQ.** Five well-written entries covering exactly the questions an ethical-fashion user would ask: data privacy, AI energy use, affiliate disclosure. Inline in settings rather than a separate page is the right call. The answers are specific and honest.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Nav accessibility is inconsistent across screens**
- **What**: `sc-feed` and `sc-notif` navs use `<nav>`, `role="button"`, `tabindex="0"`, `aria-label`, and `onkeydown` handlers. `sc-boutique`, `sc-profile`, `sc-vprofile`, `sc-create`, and `sc-postview` navs are plain `<div class="bnav">` with `onclick` only.
- **Why it matters**: Screen reader users get a fully accessible app on the home tab and a broken one everywhere else. This will fail App Store accessibility review.
- **Fix**: Extract nav into a JS render function that always outputs the full accessible markup. All copies must use `<nav>` with `aria-label`, `role="button"`, `tabindex="0"` per item, and `onkeydown` Enter/Space handlers.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden index.html`

**[P1] Create step 2 cognitive overload — primary content creation drop-off risk**
- **What**: Step 2 packs 13 type chips + collapsible color section + 10 material chips + importance slider + size labels + delete button into one scrollable form. Working memory limit is 4 items per decision group. Material alone presents 10 options with no grouping.
- **Why it matters**: This is the only path to publishing content. Every percent of friction here reduces content velocity, which reduces feed quality, which breaks the app's core value loop.
- **Fix**: Redesign as progressive disclosure. Type chips first. Material appears after type is selected. Color and size become "optional details" in an expandable. Happy path = 2 taps, not 5 scrolled sections.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout index.html`

**[P2] "New Look" hardcoded English title**
- **What**: `<div class="create-title">New Look</div>` — no `data-i18n`, hardcoded English in an otherwise fully French app with i18n support throughout.
- **Why it matters**: Breaks linguistic immersion on the most visible screen transition.
- **Fix**: Add `data-i18n="create_title"` and define the translation key ("Nouveau Look" in FR, "New Look" in EN).
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable clarify index.html`

**[P2] Developer test tool visible in production settings**
- **What**: "Test IA locale (multi-point)" in Settings — a dev tool with image picker, bounding box handles, and raw JSON output — is visible to all users.
- **Why it matters**: Makes the app look unfinished. Exposes internal tooling. In beta it is defensible but needs gating.
- **Fix**: Wrap in `if (localStorage.getItem('wa_dev_mode') === '1')` check. Unlock via tapping the version number 7 times (standard pattern).
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden index.html`

**[P2] `transition: all` on interactive elements**
- **What**: Five or more elements animate all CSS properties on interaction (camera controls, step-back button, gallery thumb).
- **Why it matters**: Triggers layout and paint on every interaction. Frame drops on mid-range Android, especially with the camera active.
- **Fix**: `transition: transform 140ms cubic-bezier(0.23,1,0.32,1), opacity 140ms cubic-bezier(0.23,1,0.32,1)` everywhere. Only transform and opacity.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable animate index.html`

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**
Walking through: opens app, scrolls feed, starts creating a post.
- No autosave signal in step 2. Casey gets a call mid-chip-selection. On return, hotspot data may be lost — HTML shows no recovery mechanism.
- Material chips are approximately 24-28px height on 375px width — below the 44pt minimum touch target. One-handed rapid selection will produce misfires.
- No swipe-between-posts gesture. Casey loses her place in the feed after every interruption.
- The `← Retour` back link on visitor profile (`back-row`) has no minimum touch target — raw `←` character at 13px. Thumb miss is near-certain.

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer)**
Walking through: new user, sees the feed for the first time.
- Hotspot `+` dots are hidden until the photo is tapped. Nothing in the feed slide teaches this. Jordan will treat WearAura as a read-only photo viewer — the entire product value proposition is invisible.
- "Lean Vision" appears in step 2 with no explanation. Jordan will wonder if it is an upsell, a premium feature, or something to skip.
- FAQ is at Settings > Centre d'aide — Jordan will never find it in the first session. There is no onboarding flow anywhere in the HTML.
- The step 2 "＋ Ajouter hotspot" button is gold on transparent with a 1px gold border. It doesn't visually match the primary `.btn` style — Jordan may not identify it as the primary action.

**Léa (Ethical Fashion User, project-specific)**
Profile: 22 years old, buys secondhand, follows sustainable brands, wants to discover responsible alternatives without being lectured.
- The ethical discovery mechanic requires tapping a specific hotspot on a specific post. There is no way to browse "only secondhand looks" in the feed. Boutique is the only ethical-first surface — but Léa came to WearAura for the social feed.
- The ambient audio element (`soundhelix.com` URL) is a test URL. If still active in beta, Léa will encounter a jarring generic background track that contradicts the atelier atmosphere.
- No sustainability signal visible in the feed slide (no CO2 footprint, no "secondhand" badge). The Lean Vision data is captured at creation time but never surfaces passively to viewers.

## Minor Observations

- The `sc-forgot` back navigation is `color: var(--gold)` — the only screen where back navigation is gold-colored. Every other back uses the standard border button. Inconsistent visual language.
- `sc-postview` uses a semantic `<button class="pv-back">` for its back button. Every other back navigation in the app uses `<div onclick>`. One semantic element in a sea of divs.
- Music picker attribution `<a href="https://www.jamendo.com">` is missing `rel="noopener noreferrer"` — minor security concern on a `target="_blank"` link.
- Ambient audio `src="https://www.soundhelix.com/examples/mp3/SoundHelix-Song-1.mp3"` is a test asset. Should not be in production.
- The About panel duplicates "CGU / Confidentialité / Politique d'affiliation" links already present in the settings legal section at the bottom. Two entry points for the same legal content adds length and confusion.
